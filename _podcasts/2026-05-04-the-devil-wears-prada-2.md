<section>
  <p>
    A nostalgia-fueled sequel that is genuinely better than it has any right to be. 
    The Devil Wears Prada 2 uses the Runway universe as a costume to tell a smarter 
    story about the collapse of traditional magazine publishing — clicks, content 
    creation, and fast fashion replacing the features and spreads that defined an era. 
    It's a real thesis, and the script earns it.
  </p>
  <p>
    Anne Hathaway is the clear center of gravity here, Meryl Streep brings more 
    vulnerability to Miranda than the original, and Stanley Tucci is reliably great 
    as Nigel. The film runs a little hyper-polished — lots of real locations, drone 
    shots, a Morning Show sheen — but it never gets in the way of a genuinely fun 
    time at the movies. The songs are a real standout and could be legitimate Best 
    Original Song contenders. Good fun. Don't let anyone tell you otherwise.
  </p>
</section>
